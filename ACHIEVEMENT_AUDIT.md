# Achievement Audit — Jumpers Football Manager

**Total achievements: 250**
**Purpose framework:** Greater depth of experience | Educating on features | Moments of joy | Post-event narrative validation

Difficulty is scored 1-10:
- **1-2** Natural / guaranteed within first match or two
- **3-4** Likely within first season of normal play
- **5-6** Requires deliberate effort or multiple seasons
- **7-8** Requires specific setups, luck, or long careers
- **9-10** Extremely rare, perfect conditions, or career-spanning commitment

## SUMMARY

**Strengths:**
- Excellent coverage of match events, scorelines, and player moments
- Strong "joy" category with fun easter eggs and surprising triggers
- Good educational onramp (first steps, training, feature discovery)
- Narrative achievements beautifully validate ticket/trial/arc outcomes
- Aspirational endgame (Sunday To The Stars, Completionist, Maxed Out) gives long-term purpose

**Weaknesses:**
- **Loan system completely uncovered** — players may never discover it
- **Club relationships underserved** — only 1 achievement for a rich system
- **Holiday mode underserved** — only 1 achievement for a unique feature
- **No squad continuity/turnover achievements** — the emotional bond of keeping players is untracked
- **GK position almost invisible** in achievement space
- **Shortlisting has no educational hook** — players may never use it

**Overall health: Strong.** The 250 achievements cover the vast majority of game systems with good difficulty distribution. The main gaps are in the transfer/relationship ecosystem and in cross-system narrative threads that would reward players for connecting different features together.
